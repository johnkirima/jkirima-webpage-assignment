About Me

Hey there! My name is John Kirima and I am a senior at the University of Iowa majoring in Business Analytics and Information Systems. I am set to graduate in Spring 2026 and I could not be more excited about what comes next. My goal is to become a Data Scientist and I have been working on side projects to build up my skills in data analysis, machine learning, and visualization.

Outside of school, I spend most of my time watching movies, listening to music, and following Manchester United. I also enjoy working on personal coding projects and learning new technologies in my free time.

Movies

I am a huge movie person. I watch about two movies a day which might sound like a lot but I genuinely enjoy critiquing films and thinking about what makes them good or bad. My all-time favorite movie is Inglourious Basterds directed by Quentin Tarantino. Everything about that movie is perfect — the dialogue, the tension, the acting. Speaking of acting, my favorite actor is Christoph Waltz. His performance as Hans Landa is one of the greatest I have ever seen on screen.

Music

When it comes to music, Hip Hop is my go-to genre. I listen to it pretty much every day whether I am studying, working out, or just relaxing. My favorite artist is Kendrick Lamar. His storytelling ability and lyricism are on another level. Albums like good kid, m.A.A.d city and To Pimp a Butterfly are masterpieces in my opinion.

Manchester United

If there is one thing that defines me outside of academics, it is my love for Manchester United. I have been a Red for as long as I can remember and supporting this club has been one of the most emotional experiences of my life. There is something about putting on that red jersey on matchday and feeling connected to millions of fans around the world. The Theatre of Dreams is not just a stadium — it is home.

My favorite player of all time is Wayne Rooney. The passion he played with, the goals he scored, and the way he gave everything for the badge every single game — that is what being a United player means. I still get chills watching his overhead kick against Manchester City. Rooney was not just a striker, he was the heart and soul of the team during some of our best years under Sir Alex Ferguson.

Even though the last few years have been tough for United fans, I will never stop supporting this club. That is what being a real fan is about — sticking with your team through the highs and the lows. I remember the glory days of winning the Premier League and Champions League, and I believe those days will come back. Glory Glory Man United!
